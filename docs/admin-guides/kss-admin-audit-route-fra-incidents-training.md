# Footasylum KSS Training Guide
## Route Planning, Audit Scoring, Fire Risk Assessments, and Incident Tracking

**Audience:** KSS Admin / Compliance users  
**Format:** Slide-by-slide training guide (can be used as a presentation script)

---

## Slide 1 — What this training covers
By the end of this session, users will be able to:
1. Plan audit routes in **Route Planning**.
2. Input and review audit scores in **Audit Tracker / Audit Lab**.
3. Create and submit **Fire Risk Assessments (FRA)**.
4. Input, manage, and track incidents from open to closure.

---

## Slide 2 — Navigation overview
From the left-hand navigation, these areas are used:
- **Route Planning** → create travel routes and optimise store visits.
- **Audit Tracker / Audit Lab** → add audit outcomes and monitor score performance.
- **Fire Risk Assessment** → create new FRA records and monitor completions.
- **Incidents** → log incidents, assign investigations, and close cases.

**Trainer tip:** Keep the session practical—ask each learner to complete one full workflow in each module.

---

## Slide 3 — Plan an audit route (workflow)
1. Open **Route Planning**.
2. Select the date/window for planned visits.
3. Add target stores (by search, region, or priority list).
4. Reorder stores to optimise route efficiency.
5. Save and share route plan with relevant users.

**Good practice**
- Group stores by geography first.
- Prioritise overdue/high-risk stores at the top of route plans.
- Use route plans to pre-brief audit priorities before travel.

---

## Slide 4 — Input audit scores (workflow)
1. Complete the store audit visit.
2. Open **Audit Tracker** (or **Audit Lab**, depending on your role view).
3. Find the relevant store/date entry.
4. Enter section-level scores and comments.
5. Save the audit record.
6. Verify score appears in tables/charts/league view.

**Data quality tips**
- Keep scoring evidence-based (match comments to observed conditions).
- Add corrective actions for low-score sections.
- Use consistent wording for recurring themes (helps trend reporting).

---

## Slide 5 — Create a Fire Risk Assessment (FRA)
1. Open **Fire Risk Assessment**.
2. Choose the target store/location.
3. Complete FRA sections (hazards, controls, findings, actions).
4. Assign action owners and due dates for each risk item.
5. Submit/save FRA.
6. Re-open completed FRA entries to monitor action progress.

**Good practice**
- Phrase risks clearly: *hazard → risk → control required*.
- Apply realistic due dates and named owners.
- Use review reminders for unresolved actions.

---

## Slide 6 — Log and track incidents end-to-end
1. Open **Incidents**.
2. Click **New Incident** and complete the mandatory fields.
3. Update severity/status as investigation progresses.
4. Assign an investigator.
5. Record actions and evidence.
6. Close incident only when outcomes/actions are complete.

## Slide 9 — End-to-end process map (trainer visual)
```text
Route Planning → Store Visit Completed → Audit Score Entered
                                ↓
                      FRA Completed (if required)
                                ↓
                Incident Logged (if event occurred)
                                ↓
            Investigation / Actions / Closure Tracking
```

Use this process map to explain that all four modules are connected in day-to-day compliance work.

---

## Slide 10 — Role-based accountability
- **Auditor / Compliance Officer:** Route plan execution, audit scoring, initial FRA inputs, incident logging.
- **Manager / Regional Lead:** Review low scores, approve actions, monitor overdue FRA/incident actions.
- **Admin / H&S Lead:** Governance, reporting quality, closure assurance, trend analysis.

---

## Slide 11 — Common mistakes to avoid
- Planning routes without including overdue/high-risk stores.
- Entering scores without supporting comments.
- Creating FRA actions with no owner or no due date.
- Closing incidents before evidence/actions are complete.

---

## Slide 12 — Suggested practical exercise
Ask each trainee to complete:
1. One route plan with at least 3 stores.
2. One audit score entry including comments and action.
3. One FRA entry with at least 2 actions.
4. One incident record from creation to status update.

Then review outputs in a 10-minute feedback round.
